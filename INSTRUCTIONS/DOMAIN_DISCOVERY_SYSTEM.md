**How we find fields we didn't know we needed.** Intelligence touches almost everything. The biggest mistakes come from blind spots — whole fields we never thought to look at. This system fights that.

## 1. The core belief

> **Every list of relevant fields is incomplete — including this one.**

So we never treat the domain list as finished. We keep a *living* map and keep expanding it.

### ⚠️ The "listed ≠ engaged" discipline *(added v1.1, from the audit)*

A long list of fields is **dangerous**, not impressive. It creates the *feeling* of coverage while most fields are never actually entered. **A field that is listed but never studied is a blind spot wearing a disguise** — worse than an omission, because the omission is at least visible.

So this map is a **work queue, not a trophy**:
- A field sitting at status "Not started" for a long time is a **red flag**, not a neutral placeholder.
- A field only counts as *covered* when it has produced a real artifact (see [`MILESTONE_AND_SHOWCASE_SYSTEM.md`](MILESTONE_AND_SHOWCASE_SYSTEM.md)) — at least an L1 explainer.
- Early **priority entries** (high-leverage fields currently dark to us): developmental psychology, cultural evolution, learning theory, chip fabrication economics, technology forecasting.

## 3. How new domains get discovered (the triggers)

We add a field to the map whenever we hit one of these signals:

1. **The "depends on" trail** — studying X, we find X secretly depends on Y. (LLMs depend on chips → chips depend on lithography → lithography depends on a few companies and rare materials.)
2. **The repeated stranger** — the same unfamiliar word keeps showing up. That's a field knocking.
3. **The unexplained gap** — a question we can't answer with current fields means a missing field.
4. **The expert's bookshelf** — when serious people in one field keep citing another, follow the citation.
5. **The analogy that pays** — when an idea from a far-off field (e.g., evolution, economics) keeps explaining things, that field earns a seat.
6. **The "who could prove us wrong?"** — which field's experts would laugh at our current view? Go ask them (their work).

---

## 4. For each domain, we track

- **Why it's relevant** (the dependency or insight that pulled it in).
- **Leverage** — how much understanding-per-hour it likely offers *right now*.
- **Status** — Not started / Surveyed / In progress / Working understanding.
- **Best entry point** — the single highest-leverage resource to begin (see [`RESOURCE_COLLECTION_SYSTEM.md`](RESOURCE_COLLECTION_SYSTEM.md)).
- **Open questions it raises** — fed into [`OPEN_QUESTIONS_SYSTEM.md`](OPEN_QUESTIONS_SYSTEM.md).

(When the work begins, each domain gets a folder under `DOMAINS/`.)

---

## 5. Prioritizing (we can't study everything at once)

A domain rises to the top of the queue when it is:
- **High leverage** (explains a lot), **and**
- **On the critical path** (other things depend on understanding it), **and**
- **Currently a blind spot** (we know least about it).

The sweet spot is a field that is important, foundational, *and* currently dark to us.

---

## 6. The recurring ritual

Every improvement cycle (see [`SELF_IMPROVEMENT_SYSTEM.md`](SELF_IMPROVEMENT_SYSTEM.md)) we ask three questions and write down the answers:
1. What field did our recent work keep *touching* but never *entering*?
2. What are we assuming someone else has figured out?
3. If our whole current picture is wrong, which ignored field is the most likely reason?
