# MILESTONE & SHOWCASE SYSTEM
**Dynamic, practical, achievable goals — each ending in something you can *show*.** This is the project's progress engine and its main defense against the #1 failure mode: building endless machinery and never producing real understanding.

`Part of: PROJECT ASI — Living Instruction System`
`System Version: 1.1 (new in v1.1) · Status: Living document · Last updated: 2026-06-20`
`Born from: REVIEWS/AUDIT_2026-06-20_v1.0.md (finding C1) + direct learner request`

---

## 1. Why this exists

A multi-year project dies in two quiet ways:
1. **System astronomy** — endlessly polishing the method, never actually learning anything.
2. **Invisible progress** — months pass with no proof you've moved, so motivation drains.

The cure is the same for both: **small, reachable goals that each end in a concrete thing you can point at and say "I made this; I understand this now."**

> **A "project" here is NOT only code.** Reaching a level of understanding *is* a project — if it produces something showable.

---

## 2. The North Star (why are we doing this at all?)

Goals only make sense pointing at something. Before milestones mean anything, we anchor them to the learner's actual purpose. This is **decision-relevance**: research with no decision behind it drifts forever.

**North Star — confirmed (v1.2): all of these together, not one.** The learner wants:
- **Pure understanding** — to genuinely *get* intelligence and AGI/ASI, for its own sake.
- **To build** — to make and contribute to these systems (agents, RL, steps toward AGI/ASI).
- **To turn it into a path** — once confident, a **career / startup / serious research** direction.
- **To form an honest independent view** — a real stance, not at the mercy of hype or authority.

> ✅ **Resolved.** Milestones serve this *whole* mix. Because it spans understanding **and** building, checkpoints alternate between **LEARN** artifacts (explainers, maps) and **BUILD** artifacts (toy systems, experiments) — see [`BUILD_SYSTEM.md`](BUILD_SYSTEM.md). The remaining tuning question (which to weight *first*) is answered per-phase, not once.

---

## 3. The mastery ladder (what "a level of understanding" means)

Vague goals ("understand neuroscience") can't be reached. We use concrete *levels* per topic — like belts in a martial art:

| Level | Name | You can… | Showable proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L0** | Curious | name the topic and why it matters | a one-paragraph "why I care" |
| **L1** | Gist | explain the core idea simply, from memory | a 5-minute written/spoken explainer |
| **L2** | Trade-offs | explain how it works *and* its limits & debates | a concept map + a "steel-man both sides" note |
| **L3** | Working | apply it to a *new* problem, or build a toy version | a small build, demo, worked example, or taught lesson |
| **L4** | Independent | critique experts and hold your own reasoned view | an essay/prediction that takes a defensible stance |

You don't need L4 on everything. **Leverage decides the target level** (see `RESOURCE_COLLECTION_SYSTEM`). Most topics: aim L1–L2. A few critical ones: push to L3–L4.

---

## 4. Checkpoints — dynamic, not calendar-locked

A **checkpoint** is a single, reachable goal: *"get topic X from level A to level B, and produce artifact Y."*

**Why "dynamic"?** We don't know in advance how long understanding takes, and rigid deadlines either rush you or shame you. So checkpoints are spaced by **progress and momentum, not the calendar**:

- The **next** checkpoint is set only when the current one is reached (or deliberately dropped).
- Its size is tuned to recent momentum: going well → reach a little further; struggling → make the next one smaller and surer.
- Difficulty stays in the **"just hard enough"** zone — reachable with real effort, never trivial, never crushing. (This is where motivation and learning are both strongest.)

**Checkpoint template (kept tiny on purpose):**
```
Checkpoint:  <short name>
Topic + jump: <X> from L<a> → L<b>
Artifact:     <the showable thing>
Why now:      <decision-relevance / what it unlocks>
Done when:    <the proof exists and passes a learning-test>
Set next:     <only after this is reached>
```

## 5. The artifact menu (what "something to show" can be)

Each checkpoint ends in **one** concrete artifact. Code is welcome but is just one option:

**Understanding artifacts**
- a **5-minute explainer** (write it like teaching a curious 12-year-old)
- a **concept map / diagram** you drew (boxes + arrows showing how parts connect)
- an **annotated summary** of a landmark resource (the 4 explanation levels)
- a **"steel-man both sides"** note on a real debate
- a **glossary contribution** (hard words made simple)

**Doing artifacts**
- a **toy build / notebook / tiny demo** (when the topic is buildable)
- a **worked example** solving a real problem with the idea
- a **teach-back** — record yourself explaining it with no notes
- a **map update** to `AGI_ASI_INVESTIGATION_SYSTEM` (a path's score changed, with reasons)

**Reality-contact artifacts**
- a **logged prediction** (see the Ledger below)
- a **question dissolved or sharpened** in `OPEN_QUESTIONS_SYSTEM`

Artifacts accumulate in a **`SHOWCASE/` shelf** — a visible, growing pile of proof. Browsing it is the antidote to "have I even made progress?"

## 6. The Prediction Ledger (so reality can correct us)

*Fixes audit finding C2: v1.0 made predictions but never tracked them.*

A prediction you don't write down can never embarrass you — so it can never teach you. Every meaningful prediction gets logged:

```
PREDICTION #__
Claim:        <specific, could-be-wrong statement>
Made on:      <date>     Confidence: <Established…Speculative>
Resolve by:   <date or trigger>
Based on:     <the reasoning / which path or domain>
Outcome:      [ open / right / wrong / partial ]   Resolved: <date>
Lesson:       <what a miss taught us — the most valuable line>
```

> **A resolved prediction — especially a wrong one — is a top-tier showcase artifact.** Being wrong *on the record* is how this project earns the right to call itself truth-seeking.

---

## 7. The momentum rule (bias to action)

> **No long stretch of work without a showable artifact.**

Concretely: if a work session (or a small run of them) produces *only* more notes, plans, or system-tinkering and **nothing showable**, that's a red flag for "system astronomy." The fix is immediate: ship the smallest possible artifact — even a rough 5-minute explainer — before going deeper.

Reading and structure are *inputs*. Artifacts are *evidence*. The project is measured in evidence.

---

## 8. Health signals — is the system actually working?

*Fixes audit finding C4: v1.0 had no way to tell improvement from motion.* A few simple, honest signals, checked at each checkpoint:

- **Production:** are artifacts appearing on the `SHOWCASE/` shelf at a steady pace?
- **Depth:** are some topics climbing to L3–L4, or is everything stuck at L1?
- **Sharpness:** are our open questions getting *more* specific over time?
- **Accountability:** are predictions being *resolved* (not just made)? What's our hit/miss pattern?
- **Reach:** are *new* domains being entered (not just listed)? (ties to "listed ≠ engaged")
- **Honesty:** when did we last record being *wrong* about something? (If never — we're not testing hard enough.)

These aren't grades. They're a dashboard for steering. A bad signal triggers a fix via `SELF_IMPROVEMENT_SYSTEM`.

## 9. How this connects to the rest of the system

- A checkpoint's artifact is produced via [`KNOWLEDGE_PROCESSING_SYSTEM`](KNOWLEDGE_PROCESSING_SYSTEM.md) and proven via [`LEARNING_METHOD`](LEARNING_METHOD.md).
- Topics and target levels are prioritized using [`DOMAIN_DISCOVERY_SYSTEM`](DOMAIN_DISCOVERY_SYSTEM.md) (high-leverage, on the critical path, currently dark).
- Predictions and resolutions feed [`QUALITY_CONTROL_SYSTEM`](QUALITY_CONTROL_SYSTEM.md) and [`OPEN_QUESTIONS_SYSTEM`](OPEN_QUESTIONS_SYSTEM.md).
- Health signals feed [`SELF_IMPROVEMENT_SYSTEM`](SELF_IMPROVEMENT_SYSTEM.md).

The milestone system is the **heartbeat** that keeps all the other machinery doing real work instead of admiring itself.
