## Core project words

- **AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)** — a machine that can handle *many different* kinds of problems about as well as a capable human, not just one narrow task.
- **ASI (Artificial Superintelligence)** — a machine *far* smarter than the best humans at essentially everything.
- **First-principles reasoning** — figuring something out from the most basic facts you're sure of, instead of copying what others assume.
- **Leverage** — getting a big result from a small, well-placed effort. (A lever lifts a heavy rock with a light push.)
- **Steel-man** — arguing the *strongest* version of an idea you disagree with (the opposite of a straw-man, which attacks a weak fake version).
- **Falsifiable prediction** — a guess specific enough that it *could* be proven wrong. Only these let reality correct us.
- **Confidence tag** — a label (Established / Likely / Contested / Speculative / Unknown) showing how sure we are.

## Thinking & method words

- **Systems thinking** — studying how parts *connect and affect each other*, not just the parts alone.
- **Reference class** — the group of similar past cases used to judge a new one. ("How did past 'X in 10 years' predictions turn out?")
- **Bottleneck** — the one narrow point that limits the whole flow, like the neck of a bottle. Fix everything else and the bottleneck still holds you back.
- **Dependency** — when one thing *needs* another to exist or work first.
- **Fact / Interpretation / Projection** — what was observed / what we think it means / what we think it implies for the future. (Each is shakier than the one before.)

## Machine-intelligence words

- **Machine Learning (ML)** — software that improves from examples instead of being told every rule by hand.
- **Deep Learning** — ML using "neural networks" with many layers; behind most recent AI.
- **Neural network** — a math model loosely inspired by brain cells, made of simple units that adjust to find patterns.
- **LLM (Large Language Model)** — an AI trained on huge amounts of text to predict and produce language (like the system writing this).
- **Reinforcement Learning (RL)** — learning by trying actions and getting rewards or penalties, like training a dog.
- **Agent** — an AI that doesn't just answer, but *acts*: plans, uses tools, takes steps toward a goal.
- **Scaling** — making a system bigger (more data, more compute, more size) to make it better.
- **Compute** — raw computer processing power; the "engine size" of AI.
- **Alignment** — making sure a powerful AI actually does what we *want* and stays safe and controllable.

## Physical & world words

- **Semiconductor / chip fabrication** — making the tiny computer chips AI runs on; extremely hard, done by very few companies.
- **Lithography** — the precision process that prints microscopic circuits onto chips; a key chokepoint.
- **Thermodynamics of computation** — the physics rules for how much *energy* any computation must cost; sets hard floors.
- **Embodiment** — having a body that senses and acts in the world; some argue real intelligence needs it.
- **Cultural evolution** — how knowledge, tools, and language improve by passing between *people over generations* — intelligence as a group effort, not just one brain.
