# OPEN QUESTIONS SYSTEM
**The live questions that pull the whole project forward.** Good questions are the engine. This is where we keep them sharp, organized, and honest about what we don't know.

`Part of: PROJECT ASI — Living Instruction System`
`System Version: 1.0 · Status: Living document · Last updated: 2026-06-20`

---

## 1. Why questions, not answers, are the center

A research system organized around *answers* defends what it already believes. A system organized around *questions* keeps reaching. We treat "I don't know" not as failure but as the most valuable signal in the project — it points exactly where to look next.

---

## 2. How a question is recorded

Each open question gets a short, structured entry:

- **The question** (sharp, specific — see [`RESEARCH_METHOD.md`](RESEARCH_METHOD.md)).
- **Why it matters** (what would change if we knew the answer).
- **Type** (see categories below).
- **Current best guess** + confidence tag, or honestly "no idea yet."
- **What would answer it** (a resource, an experiment, a prediction that resolves).
- **Status** (open / partial / answered / dissolved).
- **Links** to the domains and paths it touches.

> "**Dissolved**" is a special, prized outcome: when a question turns out to be *confused* — based on a wrong assumption — and disappears once the assumption is fixed. Dissolving a bad question is as valuable as answering a good one.

---

## 3. Categories of questions

- **Definitional** — what do we even mean? (e.g., *Is "AGI" one thing or many?*)
- **Empirical** — what is actually true in the world? (e.g., *Are we running out of high-quality training data?*)
- **Theoretical** — what is possible/impossible in principle? (e.g., *Is there a limit to what any learning system can learn from finite data?*)
- **Engineering** — can we actually build it, and with what? (e.g., *What's the real chip/energy ceiling this decade?*)
- **Causal** — what depends on what? (e.g., *Does better reasoning require embodiment — a body that acts in the world?*)
- **Normative** — what *should* happen? (e.g., *Who should control a system this powerful?*)

---

## 4. Seed questions (the starting set — to be grown and revised)

These are examples to launch the project, each tagged with type and confidence. They are *not* a complete or fixed list.

1. *(Definitional)* Is "general intelligence" a single capability, or a bundle of many that we lump together? **[Contested]**
2. *(Empirical)* How far can scaling current methods go before hitting a hard wall — and which wall (data, energy, reasoning, reliability) comes first? **[Contested]**
3. *(Causal)* Does human-like general intelligence *require* a body and acting in the world (embodiment), or can it come from data alone? **[Contested]**
4. *(Theoretical)* Are we missing a fundamental theory of *how learning should work*, the way we once lacked a theory of heat before thermodynamics? **[Speculative]**
5. *(Engineering)* What is the real-world ceiling on compute and energy this decade, set by chips, supply chains, and physics? **[Likely has hard limits — value unknown]**
6. *(Empirical)* How much of human intelligence is in the single brain vs. in *culture* — language, tools, and knowledge passed between people? **[Contested]**
7. *(Causal)* Which is the true bottleneck to AGI: better algorithms, more compute, better data, or a missing idea we don't have words for yet? **[Unknown]**
8. *(Normative + Engineering)* Can a system be made far more capable than humans *and* remain controllable and aligned — at the same time? **[Contested, high-stakes]**

## 5. How questions flow through the system

```
A question is born  ──►  recorded here  ──►  becomes an INVESTIGATION
   (from a resource,        (with type +        (RESEARCH_METHOD)
    a gap, a surprise)       confidence)              │
        ▲                                             ▼
        │                                    answered / partial / dissolved
        └──────────── new questions spawned ◄─────────┘
```

Answering a question almost always *creates* new ones. A healthy project has **more** good open questions over time, not fewer — because it's reaching deeper, not because it's failing.

---

## 6. Health check

Every improvement cycle, we ask:
- Are our questions getting **sharper** (more specific, more answerable)?
- Are we hiding from any question because it's **uncomfortable** or threatens a favorite idea?
- Which **one** open question, if answered, would teach us the most right now? (That one gets prioritized.)
