# AUDIT — Project ASI Instruction System v1.0
**A deliberate hunt for blind spots, hidden assumptions, weaknesses, missing domains, and failure modes.**

`Date: 2026-06-20 · Audits: System v1.0 · Outcome: drove v1.1`
`Method: QUALITY_CONTROL_SYSTEM "audit" + red-team ("assume this system is flawed — why?")`

> This audit follows the project's own house rule: **truth over comfort.** It attacks the v1.0 system on purpose, including the parts I just wrote. A system that can't criticize itself is not the system this project asked for.

## A. HIDDEN ASSUMPTIONS (things v1.0 quietly takes for granted)

**A1. 🔴 "Understanding the frontier from the outside is possible by reading."**
v1.0 assumes a motivated non-specialist + AI can understand what's *really* required for AGI mostly by reading high-leverage resources. But much frontier knowledge is **tacit** (lives in labs, in people's hands, in unpublished practice), **moving fast**, and **partly secret**. Reading gives you the published shadow of the field, not its leading edge.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* add a humility note + an **active/empirical component** (build toy versions, make predictions, contact primary sources) so we touch reality, not just its write-ups. Logged as a standing limitation.

**A2. 🔴 "AGI/ASI are coherent targets with 'requirements.'"**
v1.0's investigation doc *does* flag this (the "flight" analogy) — good — but the rest of the system still talks as if there's a single path/finish line to map. The framing "what is *required* for AGI" smuggles in the assumption that there's one thing AGI is.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* the definitional question is promoted to a **top open question**, and the investigation doc's "many forms, not one finish line" stance is referenced from the master instruction so it governs all work, not just one file.

**A3. 🟡 "The AI assistant is a reliable narrator."**
The entire system runs through one LLM (me). I **hallucinate** (invent plausible-sounding facts, citations, numbers), have a **knowledge cutoff** (I go stale), and tend toward **sycophancy** (agreeing to please). *Credit where due:* v1.0's `QUALITY_CONTROL_SYSTEM` already has an "AI-assisted project" section (hallucination / staleness / sycophancy checks) and an external-verification rule. The residual problem is deeper and **not fixable by documents alone**: the same AI that writes a claim also runs the check on it. A reviewer with the writer's exact blind spots can rubber-stamp.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* logged honestly as a **standing limitation**, not "solved." The only real fix is an *external* feedback loop (predictions that resolve, real sources, real people) — flagged as the top v1.2 priority. Don't let the existing checklist create false comfort.

**A4. 🟡 "Intelligence lives in a single brain/system."**
v1.0 leans on brain- and machine-centric paths. But the only species with general intelligence got there largely through **culture** — language, tools, and knowledge passed between people over generations (cultural evolution). Treating intelligence as a property of one unit may be the system's biggest conceptual blind spot.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* add **cultural evolution / collective intelligence** as a first-class domain and as an open question (#6).

**A5. 🟡 "The field changes slowly enough that a roadmap stays fresh."**
AI capability and economics move in months. A roadmap with no refresh cadence rots silently.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* add a **review cadence + staleness tagging** (fast-aging vs slow-aging knowledge).

**A6. 🟢 "A solo learner is the whole loop."**
No peers, no community, no external review. The system reviews its own work with its own blind spots.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* partial — add red-team/outside-contact requirements; *deferred:* real human peer/expert contact is recommended but can't be built into the docs alone.

## B. BLIND SPOTS / MISSING DOMAINS — *the trap of the long list*

Here is the audit's sharpest finding, and it embarrasses my own v1.0: **v1.0's domain map already *names* almost every field below.** That is exactly the danger. A long, impressive list of fields creates the *feeling* of coverage while most entries are never actually entered. A field that is **listed but never studied is a blind spot wearing a disguise** — worse than an omission, because the omission is at least visible.

So the real finding is two-part:

**B1. 🔴 "Listed ≠ engaged."** v1.0 treats its domain map like a trophy, not a work queue. There is no forcing function that turns a named field into actual understanding. *Fix (v1.1):* add the **"listed ≠ engaged"** discipline to `DOMAIN_DISCOVERY_SYSTEM` (a named field with status "Not started" for too long is a red flag, not a placeholder), and tie domain entry to the new Milestone system so fields get *entered*, not just *listed*.

**B2. 🟡 Two fields were genuinely absent** (not even listed):

| Genuinely missing field | Why it's load-bearing | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| **Decision theory & epistemics / forecasting under uncertainty** | The whole project *is* reasoning under deep uncertainty; it lacked the toolkit for doing that well. | 🟡 |
| **Data economics** (provenance, scaling laws of data, "running out" of quality data, synthetic data) | Data is a top-tier bottleneck, but v1.0 collapsed it into a one-line "Data" mention with no economic/scaling lens. | 🟡 |

*Fix (v1.1):* both added to the map. The already-listed-but-inert fields that most deserve early entry (developmental psychology, cultural evolution, learning theory, chip fabrication, technology forecasting) are flagged as **priority entries**, not just names.

## C. WEAKNESSES (present but underpowered)

**C1. 🔴 No mechanism for *visible, concrete progress* — and no practical intermediate goals.**
v1.0 is rich machinery with **no checkpoints, no deliverables, no "something to show."** For a multi-year solo project, this is dangerous: motivation dies without visible wins, and you can't tell if you're actually progressing. *(The user independently asked for exactly this — dynamic, practical, showable milestones.)*
→ *Fix (v1.1):* **new `MILESTONE_AND_SHOWCASE_SYSTEM.md`** — dynamic checkpoints, each producing a showable artifact, with a mastery ladder and a momentum rule.

**C2. 🔴 Predictions are made but never *tracked* — so they can't correct us.**
*Credit where due:* v1.0 already requires falsifiable predictions (`RESEARCH_METHOD` step 9; `AGI_ASI_INVESTIGATION` §5 "what would change our mind"). The real gap: there is **nowhere to write a prediction down, with a date, and check it later.** A prediction you don't record and revisit is a prediction that can never embarrass you — so it can never teach you. The *discipline* exists; the **accountability ledger** doesn't.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* add a **Prediction Ledger** (inside the Milestone & Showcase system): each prediction logged with a date, a confidence, and a "resolve-by" date; resolving one — especially a *miss* — is itself a showable milestone.

**C3. 🟡 No decision-relevance / "why are we doing this?" anchor.**
Research with no decision attached drifts forever and can't prioritize. *Why* does the learner want this — to invest, build, choose a career, or pure curiosity? Priorities can't be set without knowing.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* add a **decision-relevance** check to `RESEARCH_METHOD` and make "purpose/decision" an explicit, revisable input. *(Open item: confirm the learner's actual driving purpose — see questions back to user.)*

**C4. 🟡 No definition of "is the system working?"**
No metrics, no health signals. The improvement loop can't tell improvement from mere change.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* add lightweight **system metrics / health signals** (e.g., are questions getting sharper? are predictions resolving? are artifacts being produced?).

**C5. 🟢 No review cadence.** "Every cycle" is undefined — a cycle could never arrive.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* define cadence by *triggers and checkpoints* rather than a rigid calendar (matches the user's "dynamic intervals" preference).

**C6. 🟢 No maintenance plan for the docs themselves.** Over years: link rot, contradictions between files, version drift.
→ *Fix (v1.1):* contradiction-check already in QC; add a note that audits also check internal consistency. *Deferred:* automated checks.

## D. FAILURE MODES (how this system most likely dies)

Ranked by how likely they are to actually kill the project:

1. **🔴 "System astronomy" — building/polishing the machine instead of using it.** *Most likely failure.* Beautiful scaffolding, zero domain understanding. (This audit itself could be a symptom if it never leads to learning.)
   → *Fix:* the Milestone system forces concrete artifacts; a **bias-to-action rule** ("every stretch of work must produce something showable") is added to the master instruction.
2. **🔴 Confident hallucination hardening into "facts."** Wrong claims from the AI get written down, connected, and trusted.
   → *Fix:* hallucination/staleness checks; verify load-bearing facts externally.
3. **🔴 The illusion of understanding.** Reading summaries *feels* like learning; without testing it's shallow. The learner could spend years feeling smart and understanding little.
   → *Fix:* "no proof, no understood-tag" rule (already in v1.0) + milestone artifacts make understanding *demonstrate* itself.
4. **🟡 Library hoarding / roadmap hoarding.** Collecting resources or generating roadmaps that are never processed/executed. (v1.0 names library hoarding; adds roadmap hoarding.)
   → *Fix:* "a resource isn't done until processed" + milestones execute on the roadmap.
5. **🟡 Hype/recency capture.** Drifting toward whatever's loud this month.
   → *Fix:* fast/slow-aging tagging + reference-class forecasting + the anti-hype "smell test."
6. **🟡 Motivation collapse / burnout over years.** No model of sustainability.
   → *Fix:* small, achievable, *dynamically-spaced* wins (Milestone system) are partly a motivation engine. *Deferred:* deeper sustainability planning.
7. **🟢 Portability / single point of failure.** The whole project may be tied to one tool/account/folder.
   → *Fix:* plain Markdown in a normal folder keeps it portable; noted as a standing practice.

## E. THINGS v1.0 GOT RIGHT (so we don't "fix" them away)

Honest audits also protect what works:
- The **"machine that builds the roadmap"** framing is sound and rare.
- **Confidence tags** and **fact/interpretation/projection** separation are strong epistemic hygiene.
- **Steel-manning** and "present all sides" are correctly load-bearing.
- The **"flight" analogy** for AGI is a genuinely good guard against false precision.
- **Simplify language, not ideas** is the right constraint and is consistently applied.
- Treating **open questions as the engine** (and prizing "dissolved" questions) is excellent.

**Note on this audit itself:** the first draft of this document *overclaimed* — it listed predictions, AI-failure checks, and ~9 domains as "missing" when v1.0 already had them. Running the project's own `QUALITY_CONTROL` checklist on the audit caught this. The corrected version distinguishes **genuinely absent** from **present-but-inert**. That correction is itself evidence the quality-control loop works — and a live example of failure mode D2 (the AI confidently asserting something false) being caught.

**Top 3 fixes, in priority order:**
1. **Milestone & Showcase system** (incl. Prediction Ledger + health metrics) — kills "system astronomy," gives visible progress, makes predictions accountable. *(Also the user's direct request.)*
2. **"Listed ≠ engaged" discipline** — turns the impressive domain list from a trophy into a work queue, so blind spots can't hide as list items.
3. **Honest logging of the self-review limitation** — refuse to let the existing checklists create false comfort; name the unfixable-by-documents core and route it to v1.2.

**Standing limitations (honestly flagged, not hidden):** (a) the system still mostly *reviews itself* with one AI's blind spots; (b) no real human peer/expert contact; (c) no deeper sustainability/motivation model; (d) no automated consistency checks. Documents alone can't fix these.

**Biggest unresolved risk:** even after v1.1, the system mostly *reviews itself*. The single most valuable upgrade beyond v1.1 is a steady source of **external, falsifying feedback** — predictions that resolve against reality, and contact with real practitioners. That is the headline for **v1.2**.
